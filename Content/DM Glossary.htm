Glossary

Term Definition
Allocations The Allocations module contains a clear work flow to produce execution orders for the transfer of merchandise throughout supply chains at a level above Item Master defined as variant. Allocation pushes out inventory to the optimal retail store or sales channel.
Accounting Method The accounting method (cost or retail) that serves as the basis for how inventory is valued in a financial plan and for calculating certain metrics, such as Gross Margin (Maintained Markup).

Both cost and retail inventory metrics are available regardless of which accounting method is selected for a plan. There are simply differences in the way some of them are calculated.
Adaptive Allocations An extension of the Allocation module that automates the creation and allocation of localized assortments.

Adaptive Allocations determines an optimal configuration of products and uses a draft engine and data analytics for locations and products to fulfill a need that can be set through financial planning, location planning, and other cover methods.
Aggregate Level Forecast Creates a demand forecast across all sites in the company, which can be used to give a better forecast for items with intermittent sales, or gain a more holistic view of the item’s impact across the entirety of the organization.
Aggregation The combination of lower levels of data in the plan. This is accomplished through a simple summation, as with sales, sales units, and other quantitative metrics. However, the exact method of aggregation can change depending on the specific metric and how it is aggregated.
Aggregation Level A user specified parameter that defines the level at which data is pre-aggregated. This improves system performance and must be set to the lowest level of the product/site hierarchies to be worked at.
Allocations Rollup A view in the Allocations module with a pivot grid to customize allocations data and generate reports.
Allow Reservations A checkbox field in the Allocations Strategy grid. When checked, ensures that all stores that were set up to have reservation quantities receive their reserved stock quantities.
Average Unit Costs (AUC) The average price a company pays to obtain the merchandise sold during a given period. AUC is a large factor in determining profitability. If AUC increases while Average Unit of Retail (AUR) remains the same (or declines), margins and profits decrease. On the other hand, lowering AUC while maintaining AUR increases profitability.

AUC = Total Cost of Sales $ / Total Units Sold
Average Unit Retail (AUR) The average amount spent for a particular type of item, calculated by dividing the total sales by the number of items sold. Retailers use this metric to compare sales across different departments or categories. It is very important for suppliers to know an item’s AUR for the different retailers carrying their item. Retailers do the same comparison.

AUR = Total revenue (or net sales) / Number of units sold
Average Units per Transaction (AUT)

The primary Key Performance Indicator (KPI) that measures the average number of units purchased in a typical transaction value. The AUT is used as a lever to increase or decrease sales and margin. The AUT is typically combined with the Average Transaction Value (AVT), foot traffic counts, and/or conversion to determine the change in unit profitability caused by a change in strategy.

Home AUT: The average unit ticket price for the placeholder or collection in the Home Site currency. Displays the weighted average of AUT values for all future periods beneath. Home AUT is assumed to be inclusive of the Home Site’s VAT and is treated as such for all dependent calculations.

Blended AUT: The average unit ticket price for the placeholder or collection, accounting for all price conversions across different currencies and countries within the cluster. Displays the weighted average of Blended AUT values for all periods, both actualized and future, beneath.

AUT = Unit Sales / Number of Transactions
Average Weeks on Hand (WOH) Store The average number of weeks that an item is in the on-hand inventory. WOH allows allocators and demand planners to accurately allocate to stores based on weekly projected demand, calculated off a historic WOH average.

Average WOH Store = Total days on hand / number of weeks
Back Order Ratio

Back order ratio helps allocators and demand planners assess their forecast accuracy and make adjustments to reduce lost sales.

Back Order Ratio = Number of undeliverable orders / Total number of orders x 100
Beginning of Period (BOP) Helps allocators and demand planners understand their stock holding position at the beginning of the period, in which they can measure it against demand and anticipate overage or underage situations.
Bill of Materials (BOM) The comprehensive list of components required to create a product, as well as instructions required for gathering and using the required materials. The bill of materials is a centralized source of information for finished components.
Bin Best suited for items with intermittent, low demand. A fixed-quantity order methodology applies. A practical example is always ensuring that two items exist and as soon as one is sold, another is ordered to bring the stock on hand back to the set bin level of two.
Bin Max Units The maximum quantity of bin units. Bin type items must have a bin level specified in the number of units. A maximum bin unit number can be set, similar to how maximum safety stock is set.
Bin Min Units The minimum quantity of bin units. Bin type items must have a bin level specified in the number of units. When the inventory drops below this level, the item will be reordered. Like safety stock, bin levels may also be set by service levels.
Branch Plan Branch plans are created as children of a master plan. Multiple branch plans can exist beneath a master plan to allow users to separate subsets of data for easier planning among multiple individuals on a planning team. Branch plans can also be created from other branch plans, either containing the full data set for planning of different scenarios, or containing further subdivisions of data.
Calc Toggle A set of predefined behaviors for dependent metrics when a given metric is changed by the user. There are calculation toggles available for many plannable metrics.
Carrying Cost The marginal cost per period for holding one unit of inventory, typically a year (Hill, 59). Inventory carrying cost, also known as inventory holding cost, is the cost associated with holding inventory or stock in storage or a warehouse, in order to fulfill sales orders.

Carrying Cost = Avg. inventory investment X Carrying Charge

If Annual carrying charge = 25% and avg. inventory = $100,000, then the carrying cost is $25,000 per year
Causal The unusual events that cause a significant change in demand so allocators and demand planners can accurately plan for their demand adjusted for any causals.

Black Friday is a causal that retailers opt to keep in their YTD forecasts because it is a recurrent event that has expected demand from year to year. A causal that considers being de-causaled for spiked demand occurs when a famous person endorses or advertises for a product, which results in a spike in demand and sales. This is a one-time event with no clear trends, that retailers will probably de-causal to realize the true demand of the product.
Channel The particular channel where an item is sold. Example of channels are retail, outlet, e-commerce, no channel.
Classification The combination of prioritizing items based on consumption values (ABC analysis), and determining variability of demand (XYZ analysis). Classification allows you to prioritize items to have effective ordering policies.
Cluster Logical groups of store based on a variety of factors, such as site geography, segmentation, and channel. This helps organize groups of sites with the same sets of criteria to build demand for and allocate stock.
Cluster Set A collection of two or more clusters that are derived by the same set of criteria.
Cluster Set Snapshot The configuration of stores in the clusters of a cluster set at a specific point in time.
Collection A group of placeholders that can be planned as a single product. These are often referred to as styles or key items.
Collection Option How Smart Started placeholders will be sorted into new collections.
Color The color of the item. When combined with style, comprises what is known as a variant.
Comp Sales The sales that occur in stores that have been open for at least one full year. The exact timing of when stores become Comps is managed outside of the application.
Cost Accounting Method Accounting method in which inventory units are valued at the average unit cost (AUC) for each period.
Cost Price The standard cost or purchase cost of an item.
Coverage The presentation priority type that prioritizes order multiples type when fulfilling allocation orders.
Curve Mode The mathematical model used to generate a specific curve
Cycle Stock Inventory due to lot size quantities greater than one. Cycle stock is also called the lot size inventory. (Hill, 94). For instantaneous replenishment and constant average demand, the average cycle stock is Q/2, where Q is the fixed order quantity. Organizations can reduce cycle stock by reducing lot sizes and lot sizes can be reduced economically by reducing the order cost (Hill, 94).

Cycle Stock = Q/2, where Q is the fixed order quantity.
Days of Supply (Days on Hand) The effective length of time (in days) in which stock supply will be depleted to safety stock before being restocked. It is also called the effective replenishment cycle for a product.
Disaggregation The splitting of aggregated values among their lower level components (such as lower levels of a product or site hierarchy or time). Because plan values are stored at the lowest levels specified by the plan hierarchies, disaggregation is aggregation to lower levels than are shown in the current plan view.
Distribution Center The location used to warehouse and ship products.
Do Orders Exist? A checkbox field to identify if allocation orders exist for certain items indicating which items are included into the allocation strategy.
Dynamic Filter The filters consisted of dynamic conditions. Criteria is selected when setting up filters that affect the way all parameters that belong to that criteria are evaluated.
Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) The optimal order quantity (batch size or cycle stock) that minimizes the sum of carrying and ordering costs (Hill, 116). The EOQ model can help managers make the trade-off between ordering too little or too much.

End of Period (EOP) How much inventory is on hand at the end of a week, month, quarter, etc. This can be expressed in terms of cost inventory or unit inventory. Inventory EOP helps allocators and demand planners understand their stock holding position at the end of the period, in which they can measure it against demand and anticipate overage or underage situations.

Inventory EOP = Inventory BOP (Cost or Unit) – Sales (Cost sales or Unit sales)
Engine A generic term covering ToolsGroup JustEnough propriety calculations and operations relating to Allocation and Replenishment.
Excess For Redistribution Excess in inventory that can be moved from one site to another to address need and reduce the impact of lost sales.
Floor Set (Used interchangeably with Product Update) A planned and organized activity that typically occurs periodically, such as weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly, depending on the store's strategy and the type of products being sold. The goal of a floor set is to create an appealing and enticing shopping environment for customers while showcasing the store's latest products, promotions, or seasonal offerings. See Product Update.
Flow Cadence Allowed receipt schedule, defined by the user, for when a placeholder may receipt goods (e.g. every other week, once every three weeks)
Freeze Days Helps allocators and demand planners identity how much time is necessary to make changes to a supplier order.
Generic Used to define the generic product requirement at a high level and break it down to the specific components and items that the generic is made from.
Gross Margin Return on Investment (GMROI) Useful for making decisions about product profitability and where to invest inventory (stock) for the best profit per stock investment. The calculation is: Gross Margin / Average Inventory Cost
Group Forecast The forecast that contains a group of hierarchically-related items. Users can create demand forecasts for these groups.
In-stock/Stock on-hand (SOH) The on-hand units currently in inventory. Stock on-hand is valuable for allocators and demand planners to know so they can plan between demand and burn down rate of stock, and find ways to mitigate out of stock or lost sales while keeping product stock holding costs at a minimum.
Initialization The process of seeding a created plan with data from a user-specified source so planning can begin. Default seed types are Historic Sales LY, Historic Sales LLY, Forecasted Sales, and None (initializes a blank plan).
Inter-Branch Transfer (IBT) The transfer of goods from one branch or location to another branch or location in the supply chain.
Intermittency Intermittency is an important measure for demand planners to understand when they can expect consistent sales and when they can expect zero sales.

Inter-style Marginal Return Curves Marginal Return Curve between variants that is used to determine choice count based on the target productivity.
Intra-style Marginal Return Curves Marginal Return Curve between styles that is used to supplement the Inter-style curve. Used to determine how many collections, and variants within each collection is optimal.
Inventory The product that is sold at the style, color, size, and site level. It is the combination of an Item Master and a Site. It is also referred to as a SKU.
Inventory Manager The central hub to quickly drive inventory-related decisions. There is the ability to generate reports, manage inventory-level policies, and more.
Inventory Policy Determines the rules by which the replenishment mechanism operates. Inventory policies are designed to create the default settings for all inventories, but can be further manipulated for specific inventories in the Inventory Manager (workbench).
Item Master A product at the style, color, or size level. It has one more level of granularity than a variant as it includes size. Item Masters let you plan at the lowest level for allocating stock to sites.
Item Master Groups Logical groupings of item masters. Item master groups can be organized in such a way that saves the allocator and demand planner time when retrieving, analyzing, and building reports for particular item masters.
Last 3 Weeks Average Sales Revenue The average sales revenue over the last 3 weeks, which allows allocators and demand planners to review sales performance and make allocation decisions based on their last month of sales.

Last 3 Weeks Average Sales Revenue = Total sales revenue (over the last 21 days) / 3
Last 3 Weeks Average Sales Units The average sales units over the last 3 weeks, which allows allocators and demand planners to review sales performance and make allocation decisions based on their last month of sales.

Last 3 Weeks Average Sales Units = Total sales units (over the last 21 days) / 3
Last Week Sales Revenue The total sales revenue over the last week (depending on the weekly schedule). Last Week Sales Revenue allows allocators and demand planners to review sales performance and make allocation decisions based on most recent weekly sales data.
Last Week Sales Units The previous week period’s sales units, which allows allocators and demand planners to better compare between weekly sales units and order need based on sales trends and patterns.

LW Sales Units = Current bucket - 1
Layout A saved grid configuration within a view (saving which fields are shown or hidden) and in which order they are displayed.
Leftover (Stock) Any stock on hand that will not sell because the item is on or past the phase-out date.
Level Forecast A demand forecast for a specified level in the product or location hierarchy.
Location Strategy A group of sites that are the intended destination for the allocated stock. These groups of sites and stores are used to ensure that products are allocated to the correct locations.
Lock & Spread Functionality that freezes sales and related metrics at specified levels of a plan, ensuring that changes made are cascaded among unlocked levels of the plan.
Locker The stock reserved for demand of new sites. Lockers ensure that there is sufficient demand for new sites by storing stock in a locker and reserving it for the new store when it opens.
Lost Sales The sales that have been lost as a result of insufficient stock. This causes demand to not be fulfilled for a period of time.

Lost Sales = Demand - Constrained Demand - Stock on Hand (Demand = Unfiltered demand set by allocation strategy Demand Type field)
Maintained Markup (Gross Margin) Sometimes interchanged with Gross Margin. Gross Margin $ is the profit in currency. Gross Margin % is the profit (Gross Margin $) as expressed as a percentage of Sales.
Markdown Date The effective markdown date for a given product. Indicates the period of time in which a product is sold with a price reduction (often towards the end of its life cycle).
Master Plan The plans in Merchandise Financial Planning exist in a parent – child structure, with master plans being the top level parent. Master plans are not directly editable by the user. Instead, they are changed indirectly by merging approved data from branch plans.
Max Overage The explicit value allowed for an overage when determining a prepack. By setting a max overage value, overages can be limited when determining prepacks to avoid any extremely high stock holding.
Max Overage Percent
Max Pack Size The maximum number of items in each prepack. The maximum pack size can be set to limit the number of items in each prepack and have control over items included in particular prepacks.
Max Packs per Location The maximum number of packs that can be sent to an individual site. The maximum packs per location can be set to have control over which sites should receive more or less prepacks.
Max Stock The maximum amount of stock units that an allocator or demand planner wants to hold for a particular product. It is also called Maximum Stock holding Constraint.
Max Total Overage Pack Tolerance * Total Need. The maximum total overage allowed that is calculated in the Prepack Engine.

Max Total Overage = Pack Tolerance * Total Need
Mean Absolute Percent Error (MAPE) The measure of prediction accuracy of a forecasting method (trend estimation). It is a commonly used (but flawed) measure of forecast accuracy that is the average of absolute percent errors for each period.

Merge The pushing of data from a branch plan into a master plan. By default, merges are initiated automatically when a child branch plan is approved. Merging can also be initiated manually.
Method Fit In forecasting, is based on mean absolute percentage error (or MAPE), and is a number between 0-100%. Method fit helps you understand how much confidence there is in a particular forecasting method, with 100% being fully confident and 0% being not confident at all.
Min Allocation Ratio The percentage of total packs that any one prepack must have.
Min Pack Size The minimum number of items in each prepack.
Min Stock Per Size Mainly for apparel, ensures that core sizes (i.e. small, medium, large) are stocked at a minimum number of units.
Minimum Safety Stock Units The minimum of safety stock that is set to maintain a minimum stock holding position and have enough product on the shelf (i.e. minimum presentation stock).
Model Stock holding (Model Stock) The maintenance of adequate levels of stock of an item so that an adequate supply is always available for selling.
Monte Carlo Re-Trend The mechanism that provides flexibility when dealing with irregular sales patterns. The re-trending of forecasts is an important step to correctly manage the continued allocation of products as they transition from a pre-season plan to in-season. The allocations Monte Carlo Re-trend mechanism self-corrects the demand signal through the use of repeated random sampling and statistical analysis of the sample results. These simulated scenarios continue to evolve and adjust as sales data comes in and the life cycle of the product progresses, bringing a whole new element to demand signal management for the allocation of products.
Non-Comp Sales Generally speaking, this refers to sales occurring in stores that have been open for less than a full year.
Normal Filter The filters that update the cache of items based on whether they are included or excluded in criteria and filter conditions.
Normal Stocked The order policy type that treats items as a regular everyday selling item. Normal Stocked items must be continually stocked and replenished when its ordering criteria are met. These items are replenished using a fixed-period ordering methodology.
Normality The parameter that determines need from a combination of forecasted demand and customer orders. Normality determines whether to consider either value, and based on the selected enumeration sets the need accordingly. There are three defined normality enumerators.
  • Normal: Outstanding Qty Value is modeled in existing forecast. In the case where Forecast is 10 and in a Customer order of 8, Forecast Demand remains 10. If Forecast is 10 in a Customer Order of 80, then Demand is 80.
  • Abnormal: Outstanding Qty is not included in forecast and must be added to the total demand for day when considering order to recommend. Using same example from Normal, Demand is 18 and 90.
  • Ignore: Not considered at all and is excluded from order calculation and is not read into engine at all. Using the same examples, Demand is 10 for both examples.
Number of Prepacks The number of different prepack configurations allowed. The number of prepacks configuration allowed to control what items should be packed and shipped together in an organized way.
On Hand and On Order (OH/OO) The sum of stock on-hand and on-order. By using this method, allocators and demand planners can actively mitigate out of stock risks and better understand the total allocated stock between on-hands and orders.

On Hand and On Order = Stock On-hand + On-order (Prepack OH and OO included)
On Order Quantity The amount of inventory that has been ordered from a supplier (internal or external) but not yet received. On Order refers to all orders placed and pending that are in the late windows system policy.
On Order Quantity Cost The currency value of on order unit based on DefaultCostPrice. Allocators and demand planners use this metric to understand their on order quantity from a cost of goods standpoint.

On Order Quantity Cost = On Order Quantity x DefaultCostPrice
On Order Quantity Value The currency value of on order unit based on DefaultSellingPrice. Allocators and demand planners use this metric to understand their on order quantity from a selling price standpoint.

On Order Quantity Value = On Order Quantity x DefaultSellingPrice
Order Handling Lead Time The lead time it takes from getting products from distribution center shelves to the truck. The order handling lead time is based on the shipping schedule.
Order Manager A central location to create and manage orders before export. Normal supplier orders, direct supplier drop-ship orders, transfers, allocations, commitments, and distributions are controlled in a document-centric format while following workflow rules established to conform to the business requirements.
Order Multiple The multiple of units in which the stock must be ordered.
Order Multiple Rounding The rounding rules used to determine how order quantities that do not precisely align with the order multiple must be corrected. Order multiple rounding types are: Up, Down, Nearest, Down Protected, and Nearest Protected.
Order Policy Type Determines the status for the items in the filter and if they should be stocked, reordered, or set to another specific type.
Order Processing Lead Time The lead time it takes for products to get from the time of truck delivery to available Stock on Hand at the store. This includes the time it takes to unpack and stock items. Order processing lead time is an element of a compound lead time that is intended for use at a distribution center (DC). It equates to the number of days between receiving an order and the general availability of stock received that can be drawn from the DC. The values are additive to the total DC-Store lead times.
Overage Threshold The percentage overage value we are allowed to have when determining a prepack.
Overage Weight Used for error calculation. This pre-pack engine input confirms that we can have overages when determining pack sizes and the number of packs to each set.
Pack Tolerance The percent overage of total need allowed for allocating to all sites.
Parent Item The finished good item that is assembled or comprised of a specific set of component items as defined in the bill of material.
Pending Order Units The order units that are in pending status and not yet placed.

Total Pending Order Units = Prepack Pending Order Units + Pending Order Units (singles)
Period The smallest unit of time used in the assortment plan (typically, period represents a single week). It is also possible to plan in months or other periods as defined during product implementation periods. This is used where the end of a time frame is undefined, such as when Phase Out is not set for a placeholder.
Period Horizon The number of periods that an assortment plan will calculate by default.
Periods Before Phase-In The number of periods prior to the placeholder phase in date where receipts may be generated.
Phase In Date/Phase Out Date The date of the effective life cycle of a product. Phase In is often the introduction of a new product, and Phase Out is often the discontinuation of an existing product.
Pivot Grid Similar to a Microsoft Excel pivot table, pivot grid functionality lets users quickly rearrange the layout of the planning area, allowing for intuitive aggregations and groupings of meaningful chunks of data. Pivot grids are editable at any level for all plannable metrics.
Placeholder A non-specific product to which a variant is assigned. Placeholders can be worked throughout Assortment Planning as if they were variants.
Plan Basis A merchandise financial plan used as the basis for a target creation scenario
Policy Task The set of rules by which the replenishment mechanism operates.
Prepack(s) Consists of multiple units of the same stock keeping unit (SKU) or combinations of related SKUs packaged together.
Presentation Priority The priority of presentation against the demand in an allocation strategy. Presentation priority is dependent on the demand needs of each site or location strategy.
Product Filter A collection of products defined by user established criteria.
Product Group Used as a mechanism for distributing inventory outside of the organized product hierarchy.
Product Hierarchy The structure of product levels in an organization. For example, a 3 level hierarchy with the levels Category, Gender, and Class, where the categories are apparel, accessories, and footwear, the genders are male and female, and the classes include knit tops, short sleeve tees, etc.
Product Model The representation of how a product or set of products will perform over time.
Product Update The showcasing and promoting of new arrivals or seasonal products while maintaining the existing store layout and visual merchandising.
Projected Leftover The stock on hand at the end of the horizon. Used to reduce a stock holding position while avoiding lost sales.
Projected Lost Sales Units The expected lost sales units. Allocators and demand planners use this metric to proactively mitigate out-of-stock risks and prevent (where possible) lost sales from occurring.
Projected Lost Sales Value The expected lost sales dollar value based on lost sales units and default cost price. Allocators and demand planners use this metric to proactively mitigate out-of-stock risks and prevent (where possible) lost sales from occurring.

Projected lost sales value = Lost Sales Units x Cost per Unit - Assuming Default Cost Price
Projected Lost Sales within Lead Time Units The lost sales with lead time units, which is converted from lead time days. Allocators and demand planners use this metric to proactively mitigate out-of-stock risks and prevent (where possible) lost sales from occurring.
Projected Season Excess The expected leftover stock at the end of a season. This can help allocators and demand planners plan for minimal stock holding and reducing excess stock.

Projected Season Excess = Sum projected leftover in replenish status option (system policy)
Rate of Sale Calculator The Rate of Sale Calculator module models scenarios to help determine how much, in units or revenue, a particular placeholder or collection might sell per site in a given week.
Rate of Sales (ROS) The metric for allocators and demand planners to plan their forecasting around based on the rate at which sales are occurring for any particular items.

Rate of Sales = Value sales of the product, dividing by the average number of store selling, multiplied with the numeric distribution, divided by the weighted distribution.
Rationalization A process that recommends the number of placeholders and collections required to achieve a specified financial target based on user specified data.
Rebalance Order The redistribution order that is run between distribution centers (DCs). Used to remove excess or leftover stock from a DC and redistribute the stock to a DC with unfulfilled sub-branch demand.
Redistribution Order Used to create an order to remove excess or leftover stock from a store, and redistribute the stock to stores with unfulfilled demand.
Redundant (Discontinued) The items that are never likely to sell or be in demand. Redundant classification prevents items from being reordered. The Redundant Order policy type is used for SKUs that should no longer be planned for.
Relative Deviation The percent standard deviation. Often used as an XYZ classification method of ranking items based on their relative contribution to forecast accuracy and prioritizing the Workflow of the forecast exception review process.
Replenishment Cycle (RC) Indicates how often inventory must be ordered (daily, weekly, bi weekly, monthly, etc.) or how much stock to have on hand at any point in time, definable per SKU, not including safety stock, and specified in days. The replenishment cycle is always a factor of Review Period and must be more or equal. To reduce stock holding, replenishment cycles need to be kept as low as possible.
Replenishment Engine An application that processes information from the database and returns complex calculation for allocations and replenishment.
Reserved Stock The unit value of stock that has been reserved for an allocation order over a selected time frame.

Reserved Stock = Sum of StockReserved + StockPending
Retail Accounting Method Accounting method in which inventory units are valued at the average unit ticket price (AUT) for each period.
Retrend Factor (Multiplication Factor) The multiplication factor of which demand is satisfied over the total forecast.

Return Rate The percentage of products that are expected to be returned to the store.

Return Rate = Products expected to be returned / Total items sold x 100
Reuse Rate The percentage of products that have been returned and may be considered as saleable stock again.
Review Period Indicates how often inventory must be reviewed for re-ordering.
Safety Stock The recommended number of days worth of forecasted demand to keep on hand to meet the service level expectation. This is an integer number of days.
Sales by Type Sales can be planned by type in Merchandise Financial Planning, allowing for more detail about the circumstances under sales are expected to occur. Default sales types are regular, promotional, and markdown sales.
Scenario A specific set of user defined variables, including product, location, and time dimensions, that is used to achieve a probable outcome. This is relevant for target creation and rate of sale calculator.
Seasonality Curve A collection of indices across a window of time used to define the magnitude of sales in each period as it relates to the rate of sale.
Service Level On a given day, if an order is placed for immediate delivery, the desired chance that a product will not stock out before the order arrives. This should be a fraction greater than or equal to 0.5, and less than 1.
Shortfall Stock (Bin Level Order Policy Type) The number of days until the first order is scheduled to arrive. The shortfall stock days is the number of days that takes the stock on hand out of the shortfall condition.

Shortfall Stock = Bin Level - (Stock on Hand - Projected Orders over the lead-time, when greater than zero)
Shortfall Stock (Normal Stocked Order Policy Type) The number of days to first order that takes the stock on hand out of shortfall.

Shortfall Stock = Safety Stock - (Stock On Hand - Outstanding Customer Orders - Projected Orders within the current period)
Site The location entity that sells, accepts returns, or holds stock.
Site Code The warehouse, store, distribution center, or manufacturing plant code or value that uniquely identifies the site.
Site Currency The currency used at a particular site or group or sites or locations.
Site Eligible Identifies which specific sites are applicable for a specific item master or variant.
Site Groups The logical groupings of sites based on user preferences and set up
Site Hierarchy The structure of location levels in an organization. For example, a 3-level hierarchy with the levels Company, Channel, and District, where company represents all locations, the channels are retail, wholesale, and e-commerce, and districts include Northwest, Southeast, etc.
Smart Start The process of generating placeholder items from a target creation scenario, assigning them to collections, and seeding them with initial sales plans.
Snapshot A picture of the assortment plan metric values at a specific point in time. This is used as a means of comparing prior plan states with working plans.
Static Filter Filters that do not change or seldom change . They are often built on an as-needed basis, and often used for redundant tasks.
Stock Available to Allocate The available on hand stock quantity available to allocate out from distribution centers.
Stock Keeping Unit (SKU) The distinct type of item for sale (as defined in the retailer’s inventory management system). The SKU is intended as the most granular level when dealing with inventory.
Store Penetration The share of sales or store contributions for all variants (as a percentage). Store penetration allows businesses to better understand what their store sales growth will be for variants across all sites with the contribution percentage split between sites.
Style The clothing style, used for apparel and clothing businesses. Combined with color, consists what is know as Variant.
Supersession The models based on existing items that are often used for a new item that replaces an existing item. Supersessions mimic the demand pattern of the existing item and use it until the new item has enough actual historic demand or sales of its own.

A supersession is a sequential relationship of products that can replace each other. For example, product A is replaced by product B due to changes in design, form, or function.
Supplier Order The existing replenishment orders that may not be needed on the originally scheduled arrival dates and can be pushed out to a later date. The surplus order definition varies based on the order policy type used, such as normal stocked, non-stocked, bin, or redundant).
System Policy A setting configured for either a part of or the overall system. System policy settings are segregated by module, engine, or overall system and are organized by system policy type. These are typically grouped together by module, or by system specific functions or tasks.
Target Creation A process through which users can establish plan targets for specific areas of their business and determine the number of items required to meet those targets.
Target Segment A user defined subdivision of a product hierarchy level.
Target Service Level The strategic policy setting for the level of customer service to be provided to the customer for each SKU, or group of SKUs. It is a targeted measure by which businesses meet demand and stock holding requirements. Safety stock can be set as safety stock days, or service level. A high service level requires a higher volume of units in the safety stock (and higher stock holding costs).
This Week’s Recommended Order Units The recommended order unit for the current week (or remainder of the current week). Allocators and demand planners use this information to plan out orders from now to the end of the week to maintain stock position and minimize risk of stocking out. The planning is based on system recommendations.
Total Pack Weight Used for error calculation in the prepack engine to indicate that each site must receive as many packs as possible to meet the site's demand.
Total Push The demand type that actions available stock to stores from their direct primary source only. Total Push does not take action on available stock above the direct primary source. Total Push helps allocators and demand planners action on available stocks from their primary DC to stores based on long-term projections of customer demand.
Underage Weight Used for error calculation in the prepack engine to indicate that there should be no underage when determining pack sizes and number of packs to each site.
Variant The product that the client sells, at the style and color level (e.g. there is no size dimension). Variant is used in apparel and by allocators and demand planners to order and manage their demand for products in an organized and logical way.
Week to Date Sales Units The aggregated sales units from the beginning of the week until today. This allows allocators and demand planners to track rate of sales at a more tactical level and helps them understand where sales trends are occurring at a granular level.
Weeks of Supply (Weeks on Hand) The effective replenishment cycle for a product or the length of time (in weeks) in which stock supply will be depleted to safety stock before being restocked. Also known as the replenishment cycle for a product.
Window of Time A contiguous range of periods used to define the data returned in a view or an analysis.
Workspace A configuration of views, beyond the layout of a specific view.
X Days Rolling Sales The total sales over a specific number of rolling days. By looking at total rolling sales, allocators and demand planners can gain insights on sales performance trends over moving periods of time.
