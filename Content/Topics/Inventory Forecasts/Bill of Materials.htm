Bill of Materials

The way the Bill of Materials (BOM) functionality is implemented in TG JE only affects forecasting. The assembly item forecast is pushed down to the components of the BOM, based on the Ratio (units of component per assembly) and the lead time defined in the component configuration.

Components are defined in the Bill of Materials - Item Manager view (Views module) while the actual BOM assemblies are created and viewed using the Bill Of Materials Maintenance view. Both views are located in the Views module.

If configuring BOM, make sure the BOM checkbox is selected in the Forecast Effective Priority Maintenance view. For more information on this view, see Forecast policy maintenance.refer to the Administrator Panel User Guide.

The following is an example of an assembly BOMP1 composed of 2 parts BOMC1 with 1 unit and BOMC2 with 2 units. Though BOMC1 and BOMC2 are parts of an assembly they are also sold independently.

Within the Inventory Forecast module, the effective forecasts for both the assembly and components displays as follows:

Assembly item BOMP1

BOMP1 does not display a Dependent Forecast.

Component item BOMC1

The BOMP1 forecast is visible as the Dependent Forecast series with the Parent (BOMP1) forecast from Week 27 being effective in Week 26 of the component. There is an offset of the forecast by 7 days (Lead time).

Component item BOMC2

The BOMP1 forecast is visible as the Dependent Forecast series with the Parent (BOMP1) forecast from Week 27 being effective in Week 26 of the component. In this case, the quantity is double as it takes 2 units of BOMC2 to create 1 unit of BOMP1. There is an offset of the forecast by 7 days (Lead time).
