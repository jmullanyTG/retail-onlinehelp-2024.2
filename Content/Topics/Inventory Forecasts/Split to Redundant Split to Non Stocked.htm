Split to redundant or non-stocked inventory types

Redundant and non-stocked inventory types require additional consideration when considering split logic. There are four possible ways by which splits can handle redundant and non-stocked inventories:

  • Exclude – Excludes values from both history and forecast, inventory is essentially ignored.
  • Include – Includes values in both history and forecast.
  • Partial Forecast – Includes the history and split assuming all items participate in the model, but will not record the results of the split for redundant / non-stocked inventories.
  • Full Forecast – Includes the history and split to only those inventories which are not redundant / non-stocked.

Default split methodologies may be defined for redundant inventories and non-stocked inventories independently, or by forecast type, all through application of system polices.
