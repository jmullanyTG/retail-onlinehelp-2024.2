Schedules Overview

Reusable sets of schedules are created using the Schedules module. These schedules can be applied across modules. For example, the setting of supplier ordering and receiving schedules, as well as site shipping and receiving schedules.

By itself, a schedule is only used to determine a date or series of dates. The behavior of the schedule is determined by the mode in the schedule set and its application to a site or a supplier.

Schedule types control the recurrence of each schedule, as follows:

  • One-Time: A single occurrence of the schedule. The date must be greater than the current date (must be in the future) when creating a schedule.
  • Recurring: A recurring schedule with a specified frequency and duration.

Deleting schedules

Depending on the user's profile, any linked schedule can be deleted. Deleted schedules are deleted from all schedule sets.

  1. In the Schedule Set view, select the schedule to be deleted.

  2. Click Delete.

    A confirmation message displays.

  3. Click Yes to confirm the deletion.

Duration of schedules

The duration of the schedule determines the effective period for the schedule’s application.

  • Start Date: The effective start date for the schedule.
  • End Date: If an end date is known, it must be specified.
  • No End Date: Select this option if a schedule’s duration is unknown.

Determining the frequency of schedules

For recurring schedules, determine the frequency with which the schedule occurs. Each occurrence option is accompanied by specific options to determine eligibility in the period type.

Daily

  • Recurs Every _ Day(s): The frequency in days.

Weekly

  • Recurs Every _ Weeks On –: The frequency in weeks. A schedule designed to occur every week must be set to 1. A schedule designed to occur bi-weekly must be set to 2.

Monthly

Monthly schedules can be set up based on a numerical date modifier or on a day of the week.

  • Day _ of: Sets the day of the month on which the schedule will trigger. Values over 31 resolve to 31. Any values over 28 may risk not occurring in a specified month (check resolution details for confirmation).
  • The _ _ of : Sets a day of the week and the sequential occurrence of that day. The first, second, third, fourth, or last specified weekday can be chosen that falls in a month.
  • Of Every _ Month(s): The frequency in months. A schedule designed to occur every month must be set to 1. A schedule designed to occur bi-monthly must be set to 2.

Yearly

Yearly (or annual) schedules may be set up based on a numerical date modifier, a month or week of the year, or for a specific day of the year.

  • ...On Day _ of That Year: A value between 1 and 366 that specifies a date.
  • …On _ _ of That Year: A month or week and an associated numerical modifier. This field is based on the selected start date. For a start date of January 1st, select Month and 2 to set up a schedule for February 1st (with a start date of January 1st). Select Week and 13 to set up a schedule for March 20th. Weeks start on Sundays.
  • …On day _ of Month _: A specific day of the year on which to start the schedule (e.g. on day 4 of month July).
