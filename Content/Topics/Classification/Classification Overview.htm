Classification Overview

The Classification module is used to manually set up classifications for items based on the concept of inventory classification.

Inventory classification can help a company control its inventory by reducing the amount of stock on hand and increasing the inventory turnover. This makes distribution networks more efficient and lowers the overall cost. Classifying inventory also allows supply managers to set up a review schedule, check inventory levels, and establish inventory control.

The Pareto Principle (also known as the 80:20 rule) is a way of setting priorities. According to the principle, a minority of input produces the majority of results. This is certainly true in inventory systems, where a few items account for the bulk of investment and return on investment. For example, 20 SKUs might account for 80% of the revenue.

The exact split usually varies and may not be 80/20.

Maintaining inventory takes time and costs money. When there are limits on these resources, use the available resources to control and focus on the most important items in inventory. This is accomplished by giving items priorities from A (most important) to F (least important).

ABC Analysis is the principle used for classifying inventory items based on item's consumption values. A consumption value is the total value of an item consumed over a specified period of time.

The ABC Analysis principle divides items into three categories:

  • A: items are goods where annual consumption value is the highest
  • B: items are inter-class items
  • C: items have the lowest consumption value

XYZ Analysis is the principle for classifying inventory items according to variability of their demand. Understanding XYZ Analysis can helps improve the accuracy of forecasting.

The formula for calculating variability is: Relative Deviation = (Standard Deviation / Demand History) x 100.

The results from this calculation are:

Class % Relative Deviation Results
X < 50 High Stability
Y < 50 - 100 > Low Stability
Z > 100 Volatile

ABC Analysis and XYZ Analysis can help produce better classification and policy decisions.
