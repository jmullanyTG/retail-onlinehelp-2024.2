Reviewing multi-level supply chains

A multi-level supply chain with a grandparent scenario is one in which:

  • external suppliers source a primary DC
  • the primary DC sources regional DCs
  • the regional DCs source their local stores.

With this supply chain, TG JE:

  1. calculates projected demand versus projected stock on hand for each item at each store.
  2. rolls up store demand to calculate stock needed at each regional DC.
  3. rolls up regional DC demand to calculate stock needed at primary DCs.
  4. generates sufficient purchase orders from suppliers to ensure enough stock is available at primary DCs to distribute to regional DCs as needed.
  5. generates distribution orders between primary DCs, regional DCs, and stores to ensure stores have enough stock on hand to meet anticipated demand.

To review a multi-level supply chain

  1. From the Maintenance tab, click Supply Chain.

  2. Select the desired supplier using the Open Supply Chain filter and click Ok.

    The supply chain document displays.

  3. Select a primary DC.

    In the following example, the supply chain's primary source displays in the Alternate Source Name column as External Supplier.

  4. Beneath the primary DC, select a regional DC.

    For example, DC - US East with the supply chain's primary source being DC - North America.

  5. Expand the regional DC and select a store.

    In this example, the store’s primary source displays as DC - US East.
