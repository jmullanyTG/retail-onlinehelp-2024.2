Example: Introducing new items

Policy tasks apply replenishment and stocking strategy-related settings to the items contained in the task's filter.

An example would be the creation of the following policy tasks to support the introduction of a new item to stores:

  • A policy task that sets the default distribution center stocking strategy for the new item.
  • A policy task that sets the default store stocking strategy for the new item.
  • A policy task that excludes particular stores from receiving this item.

Creating a policy task for DCs

  1. From the Execute tab, click Policy.
  2. From the Policy Task toolbar, click New.

  3. In the Name field on the General tab, enter New Items (to DC).

  4. In the Filter field, click Change and select the appropriate filter.

  5. On the Policy tab, complete the fields as required.

  6. Click Ok.

    The policy task is created and the displays on the Policy Task grid.

Creating a policy task for stores

  1. From the Execute tab, click Policy.
  2. From the Policy Task toolbar, click New.

  3. In the Name field on the General tab, enter a name for the policy task.

  4. From the Filter field, click Change and select the appropriate filter.

  5. On the Policy tab, enter the following values:

    • Order Policy Type: Normal Stocked
    • Review Period Days: 1
    • Replenishment Cycle Days: 7
    • Safety Stock Days: 14
    • Minimum Safety Stock Units: 2

  6. Click Ok.

    The policy task is created and the displays on the Policy Task grid.

Creating a store eligibility policy task

  1. From the Execute tab, click Policy.

  2. From the Policy Task toolbar, click New.

  3. In the Name field on the General tab, enter Item Name.

  4. From the Filter field, click Change and select the Item Name filter.

  5. In the Order Policy Type field on the Policy tab, enter the appropriate order policy type.

  6. Click Ok.

    The policy task is created and the displays on the Policy Task grid.
