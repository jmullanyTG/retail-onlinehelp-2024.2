Best practices in promotions planning

Promotions Planning is designed to provide a great deal of flexibility when modeling promotional effects for products within plans. However, there are best practices that can maintain a high level of efficiency and help users get the most out of this area of Assortment Planning.

Using defined promotions as templates

As promotions do not have ties to specific products or levels of the site hierarchy, the same basic promotion can be used repeatedly for other areas of the business.

In the above example, the same base promotion has been applied to three distinct product areas, including different cluster sets, and start and end dates.

The same AUR effect and behavior is applied to all applications. Aside from that, as long as applications occur within the eligibility start and end dates (or the Is Always Eligible? option is checked), users retain a great deal of flexibility with the specifics of how the promotion is applied to each area.

This same methodology is mirrored to an extent within the details of each application as well. Start and end dates, final indices, Hold Sales options, and even participation in the promotion application can be varied at the placeholder/cluster level. Making use of the ability to make changes at the application and detail levels helps minimize the number of promotions users need to create and maintain.

Using the Is Always Eligible? option

The Is Always Eligible? option ensures that multiple frequently used standard promotions are always available for selection.

For example, where 20% off promotions are frequently used. In the above example, one promotion with a 20% off Promotion Discount with Is Always Eligible? checked is created.

Users can then apply that promotion with different final indices to different products and site hierarchy levels without ever have to specify or extend eligibility start or end dates or create duplicate promotions to achieve the same effect.

Holding sales versus not holding sales

The Hold Sales option allows users to choose whether a given promotion application can change the overall magnitude of a product’s sales plan or simply shift the magnitude of sales in each period while still adhering to the original sales plan.

An example of the Hold Sales option unchecked

After activation of the promotion application, which has a Final Index of 1.5 on each cluster, the Ttl Sales (U) has increased to 41,963 and ROS U to 248.30. This is because the Hold Sales option is not checked for either cluster the product is assorted to allowing the overall sales plan to increase.
