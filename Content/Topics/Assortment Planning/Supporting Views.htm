Placeholder and Collection supporting views

The supporting views are intended to give the same access to all elements of the assortment plan while minimizing time spent scrolling and loading custom views. All right, left, and bottom pane supporting views can be quickly expanded, hidden, docked, floated, and configured on a single screen or multiple screens.

Displaying or hiding view options in Placeholder and Collection views

System policy Hidden Items on the View Selection dropdown (Module: Assortment) defines which view options display in the View Selection parameter dropdown.

Showing supporting views

Move the cursor over or click the tab corresponding to the desired view (which can be located at the left, right, or bottom of the screen) while in Placeholder or Collection view to view it. Click outside the view elsewhere in the screen to auto-hide it.

Views can be sized by hovering the cursor over the edge of the view, changing it to a cross-hair, and then dragging to expand or contract.

Docking, hiding, and floating

Once a view is showing, click in the upper right hand corner to dock it in the side of the screen it appeared.

Right click the header area of a view to access the view options menu:

  • Auto-Hide hides the view and returns it to pre-docked behavior
  • Float transforms the view into a floating window which can be sized, docked in other areas of the screen, or moved to another screen
  • Dock docks the view in its last docked position (this is only available when the view is floated)

Advanced screen configuration

Engage the screen configuration helper by clicking the header of any docked or floated view and dragging. While holding the left mouse button, hover over the arrows to see the target locations of the view. Release the left mouse button while hovering over the desired arrow to dock the view in the location shown.

Views can be dragged to the far left, right, top, or bottom of the screen and layered over other views. A dock assist outline shows the resulting position of the view when the left mouse button is released.

Lastly, it is possible to right-click, float, and position individual tabs in this manner allowing as many supporting views as desired to be displayed at once.

Screen configurations can be saved using the Workspaces feature in the toolbar.
