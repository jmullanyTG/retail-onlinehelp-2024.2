Validating a supply chain

A valid supply chain can be used to split inventory among DCs. For a supply chain to be valid, the sites and DCs in the supply chain must match the sites and DCs in the plan, and all sites in the supply chain must have a DC as their primary source.

Only supply chains for master plans can be selected and validated. To see whether the supply chain defined for a master plan is valid, validate it from the Maintain Financial Plan window. For convenience, the result is passed down to its branch plans.

Viewing supply chain status within the Financial Plan Maintenance window

  1. Navigate to Plan > Merchandise Financial Planning.
  2. Click Manage on the Financial Planning toolbar.
  3. Select a plan.

The Stock Split Supply Chain and Supply Chain Status fields appear in the Settings tab.

(If a branch plan was selected, these values are inherited from its master plan.)

If the supply chain is not valid (because it has not been defined or has errors), its status value is colored red in the plan's Settings/General tab.

To validate a supply chain:

  1. From the Financial Planning toolbar, click Manage.

  2. In the Financial Plan Maintenance window, select a master plan and click Edit.

    The Maintain Financial Plan window displays.

  3. Navigate to Supply Chain Status field in the General tab.

  4. Click the check mark on the right-side of the Supply Chain Status field to validate the supply chain.

    The result of the validation appears in the Supply Chain Status field.

Results

  • If the Supply Chain Status value is Valid, the supply chain is used to split inventory among DCs.

  • If the Supply Chain Status value is Unknown or Invalid, inventory is split generically among DCs.
