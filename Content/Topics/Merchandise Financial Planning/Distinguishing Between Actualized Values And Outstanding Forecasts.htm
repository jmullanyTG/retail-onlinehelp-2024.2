Distinguishing between actualized values and outstanding forecasts

Planners can separate sales and receipt information for selected calendar levels into aggregated actualized and outstanding portions using the Remaining/Elapsed metric within the Hybrid Financial Planning Grid. Using this metric, trends relating to actual sales versus planned sales can be easily distinguished from the balance of a forecast. For receipts, it allows planners to see what has already been received against what is expected to be received in the future within a time period.

The metric can be used in the Filter, Row, and Columns in the pivot grid. In the Filter area, users can choose either Elapsed or Remaining or both. This can be useful if deliberately planning only future periods or analyzing past periods,

However, the recommended and probably the most common usage for this field is in the Column area (the same area as time periods), and before Week. This allows visibility to the portion of the period which has actualized, and the portion which is still outstanding or remaining, with subtotals. The metric position could be higher to allow for subtotals such as quarter-to-date or year-to-date.

Positioning of this metric within the Column area is important as the split between Remaining and Elapsed happens within the period of time above or to the left of the Remaining/Elapsed metric.

For example, if Remaining/Elapsed is positioned after Year, then elapsed periods of time within the year split into the next calendar level down (such as Quarter or Month). Elapsed periods of time subtotal as Elapsed Total and periods of time remaining subtotal as Remaining Total.

When the Remaining/Elapsed metric is positioned after Quarter or Month, the Elapsed Total and the Remaining Total are constrained to each quarter or month.

In the below example, M05 is entirely Elapsed, and the monthly total is seen in the Elapsed Total field. M06 is partially Elapsed and partially Remaining. The two totals combine to equal the value in the M06 Total field. M07 is entirely Remaining meaning all its values are aggregated to the value in its Remaining Total field.

When the Remaining/Elapsed metric is placed after the lowest calendar level (Week), each period has a header indicating the status of that particular period. There are no subtotals at the lowest calendar level.
