Using keystroke shortcuts to reduce data entry

Use keystroke shortcuts when entering large numbers. Selecting one of the shortcuts inputs a series of zeros (with any required separating commas) behind an entered numeric value. For example, entering the number 5 and pressing K inputs 5,000.

Keystrokes can be combined. For example, 2KK results in 2,000,000 and 3CK results in 300,000.

The shortcuts are:

Shortcut Expression
C 00
K ,000
M 000,000
B 000,000,000

Zeros can be entered directly.
