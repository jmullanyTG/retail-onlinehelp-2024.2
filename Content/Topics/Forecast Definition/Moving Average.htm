Moving average

One way to modify the influence of past data on the mean-as-a-forecast is to specify at the outset just how many past observations will be included in the mean. This procedure is described as a moving average because as each new observation becomes available, a new average can be computed by dropping the oldest observation and including the newest one.

When the moving average method is applied, the forecasting engine will look back a specific number of periods, find the mean of those periods and then forecast a straight, flat line at that value for all future points. The number of points the engine observes is the period of the moving average.

For the series of data points 7, 4, 10 representing historical sales and a 3 point moving average (looking at the last 3 points), the moving average is 7 (the mean). When the sales figures for the next month come in with sales of 9 units, then the moving average takes the data points of 4, 10, 9 into account and presents a new forecast of 7.66.
