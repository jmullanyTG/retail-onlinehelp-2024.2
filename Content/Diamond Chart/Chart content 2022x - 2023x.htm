ToolsGroup JustEnough New and Improved Functionality by Release

Wholesale Planning

As the presence of online marketplaces expands, traditional retailers are identifying a need to create plans for these non-traditional sites. This problem statement could begin with a retailer allocating stock (and sales expectations) to be fulfilled by Amazon, or Walmart, or the like; or it could expand into a broader "wholesale" strategy. In order to support this growing need for retailers to plan in the wholesale space, the traditional TG JE retail functionality has been enhanced to better support wholesale functions.

While wholesale is often treated as a separate business entity, there is often a need to roll-up retail and wholesale plans into a singular total company view. As such, Wholesale functionality within TG JE is embedded beside Retail functionality. Rather than creating separate plans for Wholesale and Retail, the TG JE structure supports wholesale as a channel within the total company plan. This allows for easier movement of planned stock, sales, and other metrics as demand may shift from one channel to another, as early as the planning process. By composing the TG JE module to treat Retail and Wholesale as parts of a Total, revisions during the execution phase are also easier to implement.

These Release Notes are crafted to keep Wholesale-related functionality grouped together; structured as a logical progression through the planning and execution processes. It is important to keep in mind that the enhancements noted in this section, are also visible in the standard retail modules, as applicable.

Establishing Wholesale Customer to Wholesale Account Relationships

Using customer orders as sales history for Customers

From the Wholesale perspective, “sales” can be viewed in two ways within TG JE:

  1. Sales (or orders) signifying stock movement from the Wholesaler to the Customer Accounts, are referred to as Sell-In

  2. Sales made by the Customer Accounts to end-consumers are referred to as Sell-Out

There is reliable historical data for Sell-in across all accounts. Sell-out is not as consistently available. To that end, these accounts use Sell-ins (customer orders) in place of sales history for forecasting purposes and are managed by grouping multiple Sell-ins together into major accounts.

A new weekly task, (WS) (Weekly) Generate Sales History from Customer Orders, uses customer orders that happened in the past as sales history if the customer order went to an account. The relationships defined in Customer Manager determine how Customer order data rolls into which accounts.

Actualized Sell-In Sales are populated by Customer orders (with accounts being collections of customers); which adhere to the Customer/Account configuration. Customer orders with dates in the future are ignored.

Forecasting Wholesale Account Demand

Wholesale planners managing wholesale accounts can have accounts that split demand between multiple DCs. This functions similarly to TG JE's demand transference, though in this case wholesale accounts transfer their sales into DCs and not stores with demand aggregating to DCs only.

Only the major accounts are forecast and replenished. For example,

  • Vandelay Industries (the primary wholesale account)

    • BrandA

    • BrandB

    • BrandC

    Vandelay Industries is the major account and is the account that is forecast and replenished.

For TG JE v2023.4, the Customer Account Manager module was renamed Customer Manager to reflect updates that support Wholesale planning. The enhanced functionality means sites can be set up as a new site type called Account that represents a collection of customer accounts in a many customers to one Account site relationship. Customers are grouped 1 to 20 (like other entities, such as Site Groups 1-20) that can be used to show a hierarchy. Users maintain the Customer entity and do the linking to the Account site entity in Customer Manager.

Accounts are not directly replenished. All forecasted account demand is replenished at DCs where the stock is held until anticipated wholesale account purchases. The DC responsible for holding stock is defined in the Site Account Source Site Contribution widget in Site Manager.

Two new fields Account Name and Location Count located under the Customer Account group can be added to the top grid in the Customer Manager Document. Both fields are editable and have the Multiple Changes right-click menu option. The value in the Location Count field can be used within Assortment Planning.

Reviewing the customers under an Account site type and setting default contributions

Wholesale planners can, when managing wholesale accounts, use Site Manager to see the customer names for sites of type Account by selecting a new blade Site Account Customer Relations. The customer names are read-only as changes to the Customer Account relationship are performed in Customer Manager.

Sites that are not of site type Account do not display any results.

A second new blade in Site Manager, Site Account Source Site Contribution, displays a cross product of items in the hierarchy and all DCs. The blade displays, as a calculated percentage, the default contribution of each DC with the calculation being performed by new job task Calculate Source Site Percent Contribution for Accounts which looks at an account’s sales history (customer orders). Users can use the Site Account Source Site Contribution blade to refine contribution percentages as an override to set what percentage of an account’s projected stock each DC should hold. Overwritten values take priority over the calculated percentage.

After a user enters a value in the Override field and clicks Save, the Clear Override field for that row is formatted orange. The Clear Override functionality clears overrides for all the DCs for the item. When overrides are cleared the default contribution percentage is used.

If 10,000 units have a 50/50 split between 2 DCs out of a total of 4 DCs, then 5,000 units are sent to each of the 2 DCs.

A new system policy Site Wholesale Account Distribution: ItemMaster Hierarchy ID defines the hierarchy used. (For more information, see the System Policies section.)

Merchandise Financial Planning

As noted, rather than creating separate MFP plans for Wholesale and Retail, the TG JE structure supports wholesale as a channel within the total company plan. This allows users to include sites with a site type of Account. The channel name will be whatever name is included in the Site Group 1 - 20 metadata.

The wholesale channel can be included in a hierarchy, along with sub-channel hierarchies beneath the wholesale channel, for use in individual plans by account.

Including wholesale totals in the hierarchy

If the desired functionality is to include wholesale accounts in MFP but not plan them individually, then all that is necessary to do is include the Channel group in the MFP hierarchy configuration (or whichever Site Group indicates which sites are Retail and which are Accounts). Then the wholesale channel will be available when hierarchies are generated.

Including Individual wholesale accounts in the hierarchy

To include wholesale in the Channel hierarchy, the following steps must be taken:

  1. One of the Site Groups 1 - 20 should be used to represent each wholesale account. For example, Site Group 7 is given a friendly name such as Wholesale Account.

  2. Import Site Group values that exactly match each Account, and assign that SiteGroupID value to each wholesale account site.

    All Retail sites / DCs / other sites will have value of 0, No SiteGroup.

  3. Include the Site Group level in the Global Groups configuration in MFP.

Users can then view individual wholesale accounts in the MFP hierarchy.

Supply Constraints

A high-level goal of ToolsGroup JustEnough is to capitalize on Sales (Demand), with an assumption that orders can (and will) be made to achieve the necessary supply (or stock). However, individual suppliers do not have infinite capacity to manufacture stock or fulfill orders. With these capacity constraints in mind, TG JE v2023.4 introduces functionality to define supplier capacity, based on a defined Construction Type attribute. These capacity constraints can then be applied during order generation, by using a Supplier Constraint order macro. This functionality is available during both Wholesale and Retail order generation.

Assigning Supplier capacity through Attributes Supplier Manager

A new import associates suppliers with Attributes. The import fields are:

  • ConstructionCode

  • SupplierCode

  • Ranking

  • ActiveFlag

A construction code can be associated with multiple suppliers. For example, if the construction code is SHIRT then there can be multiple suppliers that can manufacture construction code SHIRT. While the construction code is very specific, the actual supplier constraint is an Attribute, which can be any Attribute.

Ranking is the list of suppliers that sets the order in which suppliers are used before consuming the next supplier’s capacity. It is possible to have different rankings per construction type. For example, for shirts, supplier A is ranked 1 and supplier B is ranked 2. Whereas, for ties supplier B is ranked 1 and supplier A is ranked 2.

Each supplier can have multiple construction types and multiple construction groups associated with them. For example,

  • Supplier 1: (V-necks, T-shirts, and Tank Tops)

  • Supplier 2: (T-shirts, Dress-shirts, and Collar Shirts)

Construction type can be based on any finite capacity such as material, machinery, time required to complete manufacture of required amount of items, and so on. Said another way, construction type is something associated with the constraint that a supplier has in terms of the material, fabric or other factor related to the ability to actually run the production of the product and what limitations the supplier has.

New system policy Supplier Constraint: Construction Type (Module: Order Manager) defines which Attribute to use for supplier constraint.

Should a product not have an Attribute associated with it then the new macro, Apply Supplier Production Limitation, available for Supplier orders in Order Manager, will not be applied and supply will not be constrained. The macro requires a product’s Attribute to be associated with a construction type in order to constrain by that construction type. Without that association, there is no link to the particular macro and supply cannot be constrained.

The Attribute needs to be set prior to creating purchase orders.

Reviewing suppliers associated with Attributes

A new blade labeled Supplier Capacity Report has been added to the main view in the Supplier Manager module. Selecting a supplier in the top grid refreshes the blade to display the Attributes associated with the supplier. Within the Supplier Capacity Report, users define the production capacity (what the supplier can make and how much they can make) for each Attribute for a time period. Construction type is an Attribute of the supplier and is also an Attribute of items. Construction type is not a level of the hierarchy.

The supplier and the construction types that the supplier can provide are visible in a new blade within Order Manager labeled Supplier Capacity Report.

Specifying capacities in time periods

Additionally, a second blade, Supplier Construction Type Capacity, has been added to the Supplier Manager module. This module shows a cross product of time buckets and construction type of the selected supplier. Users should use this module to define the production capacity of a supplier for the given time bucket. If a time frame is left unedited, it is assumed the supplier has an unlimited capacity for the given time frame for that construction type.

New system policy Supplier Constraint: Bucket Type (Module: Order Manager) defines the time period to use for the supplier constraint. The bucket range is based on today and the value for existing system policy Future Days (Engine: Forecasts).

Only the Total Supplier Quantity field is editable with all other columns being read-only. Planners can multi-select cells in the Total Supplier Quantity field to specify the capacity in that bucket.

Supplier production constraints

When an order line is selected, the new Supplier Capacity Report blade, which displays when the Details toolbar button is clicked, populates with all the suppliers that have the order line’s construction type. Planners can review how much production capacity is left for each supplier of the construction type and how much of the capacity is taken up by pending stock. Supply planners can also manage scenarios where all suppliers are unable to fulfill the order so that exceptions are managed effectively.

Often times a wholesaler’s constraint lies with supplier production. In this scenario and bearing in mind each supplier can produce X amount of construction types a month, a supply planner:

  • looks at all the unconstrained orders today

  • adjusts the order quantity based on constraints such as production capacity or orders for a particular vendor have been particularly low.

By default, the Supplier Capacity Report displays recommended orders for today without any supplier constraint applied. Planners can review and apply supplier constraints to recommended orders as needed by applying the supplier production limit of a construction type to the values in the User Order Quantity field in the top grid using a new macro, Apply Supplier Production Limitation. The values update automatically with the results displaying in the Supplier Capacity Report blade.

Secure Single Sign On (SSO) with Auth0

For ToolsGroup JustEnough v2022.3 onwards, ToolsGroup JustEnough has selected Auth0 as its Single Sign On (SSO) module. Auth0 is an authentication and authorization platform that provides a highly secure environment in which users sign-in with their email address and password or choose to continue if they have already signed in to another application such as Gmail or Microsoft Office 365. This eliminates the need for repeated system sign-ins for an improved user experience and simplifies the management of user names and passwords for organizations.

Auth0 verifies whether users are authorized to access ToolsGroup JustEnough using credentials provided by third-party Identity Providers such as Google, Okta, or Microsoft. All user management including the configuration and authentication of email addresses is handled by Auth0 while passwords are managed by either Auth0 or the third-party Identity Provider.

Users are presented with Auth0's Universal Login when signing-in.

Auth0's Universal Login is customizable allowing organizations to display their own logo and colors as well as any desired wording along with the ability to offer one or more integrated SSO modules.

The full capabilities and benefits of Auth0's Universal Login and SSO functionality can be reviewed by following this link: Single Sign On.

v2022.1 introduces the new Inventory Level Archive (ILA) data store to the Merchandise Financial Planning and Assortment Planning modules.

Reducing performance consequences and storage costs of real-time data aggregation

Assortment Planning and Merchandise Financial Planning (MFP)

Throughout the modules, in particular planning modules, it is useful to have a record of what stock units were on hand in stores for dates in the past. This gives insight into stock holding history for comparison and lets users know what the most recent stock position was as of last week, so that future weeks' stock positions can be calculated based off demand and receipt plans.

This data store is referred to within ToolsGroup JustEnough as an Inventory Level Archive (ILA) and there can be performance consequences and storage costs when the ILA data store contains too much history for too many items. This is particularly true for large-volume customers where repeated aggregation of static ILA data is time-consuming and the volume of data itself consumes considerable space.

To address these issues, v2022.1 introduces a new variant-location ILA aggregate to the Assortment Planning and Merchandise Financial Planning modules. The new variant-location ILA aggregate reduces the incidence of real-time aggregation, thus, reducing performance consequences and storage costs of repeated aggregation of static ILA data.

The Assortment Planning and Merchandise Financial Planning modules do not need stock on hand history at such a granular level as SKU-location; variant-location is perfectly sufficient for these modules. By summing up the SKU-location level ILA table into a variant-location ILA aggregate, systems can reduce storage space in their databases and reduce performance time when modules use the aggregated data store rather than the detailed one.

Configuring which ILA data store to use

Two new system policies (one for Assortment Planning and one for Merchandise Financial Planning) determine the source of stock on hand data to use during actualization. By default, the system policies are set to Inventory with the data source being a SKU-location level ILA table. Setting either or both system policies to Variant lets customers take advantage of a variant-location ILA aggregate for the relevant module.

The new system polices are:

Module System policy
Assortment Planning SOH Actualization Source
Merchandise Financial Planning Stock On Hand Actualization Source

For more information on the above system policies, refer to the System Policies section.

Upgrading to v2022.1 from a previous version

If upgrading from a previous version, there is no need to re-import data for the aggregation to run. Simply run the aggregation as a one-off after the upgrade to establish an aggregate ILA. Afterwards, whenever new imports are run, the newly imported values will be maintained going forward (assuming one of the system policies is set to Variant).

However, data can be reimported if desired. There does not yet exist a direct import into the variant-location ILA aggregate table; only the existing SKU-location ILA import continues to exist. This import has been changed to append processing to automatically cache imported SKU-location data into the variant-location aggregate. However, in order for the automatic caching to occur, one of the Stock On Hand Actualization Source system policies needs to be set to Variant.

It is strongly suggested to break the initial data import into smaller pieces, rather than trying to import all the data at once. This helps minimize the risk of the import failing.

Impact to historical EOP values of changing the data source

Historical EOP values can vary slightly depending on whether the data source is the existing SKU-location level ILA or the new variant-location ILA aggregate. The difference has to do with how the system looks backwards over time to find the stock on hand for an item.

In the following example, the recorded EOP for the period wk08 (which is from 17 February to 23 February) differs slightly between the SKU-location level ILA and the variant-location ILA.

Example

Existing SKU-location level ILA

Assume the following records exist in the SKU-location level ILA:

Period EOP Units
1 February 100
4 February 120
9 February 130
28 February 90

Currently in MFP and AP, there is no look back period when using SKU-location ILA data for historical stock on hand values. An ILA record between 17 February and 23 February is queried and the last record is chosen. Since there is no record in these dates, EOP is reported as 0 units.

Variant-location ILA

The dates used for variant-location ILA data is regulated by the system policies that maintain SKU-location ILA data (by default, the calendar used for data retention is to keep daily records for a month, weekly records for 2 years, and monthly data beyond that). Further, when variant-location ILA data is populated, it uses a look-back period to find the last record present for an inventory, not just the last record to occur within the week or month. Thus, weekly records are created, and a record for 23 February is created. The result is the records in the variant-location ILA aggregate will look more like this:

Period EOP Units
2 February 100
9 February 130
16 February 130
23 February 130
28 February 90

In this scenario, the look-back logic used to populate variant-location ILA data means that there is a weekly record to be found by MFP or AP when looking for an EOP value; a record is found in the period between 17 February and 23 February, so the application will report 130 EOP units.

Thus, it can be expected that historical EOP values will likely be higher when Variant mode is enabled for SOH actualization, because it is accounting for data that was previously omitted.

Previously, calculating Actual EOP DC Split included On Order data, but this was incorrect as On Order is separately tracked. Now, On Order is no longer included as part of Actual EOP DC Split.

Also, the metric Available On Hand (Available On Hand = On Order + Stock On Hand + Reserved - Pending) was put into the Actual EOP DC Split value.

Target Choice Counts

ROS extrapolation updated to avoid crossing zero

Within Target Choice Count/Creation, previous functionality was to rationalize the comparison ROS values and make placeholder ROS recommendations to the point where additional recommendations are not mathematically justified. Beyond that point, if a user wished to add additional placeholders, the logic was to create a decreasing ROS slope to be applied against additional, user-requested recommendations. A function of the previous slope method was that the slope could reach or cross a zero (or negative) ROS.

The logic used to create the extended slope for ROS values to be applied against additional placeholders has been updated in such a way that it will never reach or cross zero. This allows users to create additional placeholders and receive suggested ROS values for each placeholder, regardless of the number of additional placeholders being requested.

Creating a large amount of additional placeholders could result in very small ROS values for some placeholders. This is a function of a decreasing ROS slope.

Real-Time Retail (RTR) batch reduction

TG JE v2023.2 introduces functionality that allows those customers who are unable to action Real-Time Retail (RTR) recommended orders on the same day to set the date for generating recommended orders to the following day. This enhancement enables the RTR functionality to better reflect real-world scenarios.

The new system policy Generate Orders Starting Tomorrow or Today enables the generation of RTR recommended orders for the following day when the policy is set to Tomorrow (default). By setting the policy to Tomorrow, customers also gain the flexibility to reduce their nightly batch run times.

Batch exclusion for recent recommendations

Under the default setting, if an RTR recommended order was generated within the last 18 hours, and Generate Orders Starting Tomorrow or Today is enabled, the system skips generating new recommended orders for related items. This allows customers to avoid wasted time running unnecessary batch calculations within a short time frame.

Existing functionality

When Generate Orders Starting Tomorrow or Today is set to Today orders generated will NOT be excluded from the nightly batch process.

Benefits of setting the system policy to the default

When the system policy is set to Tomorrow, customers can still take advantage of Real-Time Retail instant decision making without having to wait for the nightly batch process. This reduces their dependency on the nightly batch and offers the following benefits:

  • Faster Order Processing: Customers can process tomorrow's orders today if their supply chain processes allow, facilitating quicker fulfillment.

  • Reduced Nightly Batch Needs: By generating recommended orders for the following day, customers can minimize the number of nightly batche runs, optimizing system performance.

Configuring the time elapsed since last calculation for batch exclusion

New system policy Time Elapsed Since Last Calculation for Batch Exclusion allows customers to reconfigure the default 18-hour time limit. Customers can specify the number of hours for batch exclusion, although it is not recommended to exceed 24 hours.

New job tasks

To facilitate the recommended order generation and batch exclusion process, the following new job tasks have been added:

  • (AL, RP) (Daily) Enable Batch Exclusion Process

    Description: Enable the batch exclusion process to skip generating new recommended orders for items with recent recommendations.

  • (AL, RP) (Daily) Disable Batch Exclusion Process

    Description: Disable the batch exclusion process to allow the generation of new recommended orders for all items.

To effectively implement the recommended order generation and batch exclusion process, the Enable Batch Exclusion task needs to run before the Ordering Engine Executions. Disable Batch Exclusion should run immediately after. The reason being if Enable Batch Exclusion is left on, then orders generated by users during the day would be excluded as well.

2022.4

Responding real-time to changes to stock on hand (SOH)

As of TG JE v2022.4, planners can now receive a real-time data feed from ToolsGroup Onera that reports any changes to the stock on hand (SOH) value of an inventory. TG Onera receives feeds from client ERP systems, and then feeds real-time data via a Webhook into TG JE.

To take advantage of this functionality, TG Onera is required and an API key from TG Onera configured to access and authenticate with TG JE.

The value provided by TG Onera is the total remaining SOH at that time and not the delta between the original and new values.

The integration of a continuous data feed means users can be notified in real-time that a fast selling item is approaching its defined end of period safety stock level allowing them to react accordingly. This gives planners the ability to avoid unexpected stock outs and lost sales while also avoiding over ordering stock. The result can be significant cost savings.

The real-time data feed is in addition to, and faster than, the existing nightly batch process as users don't have to wait overnight to update SOH values. It is important to note that the data feed does not replace the existing overnight batch process and still relies on the nightly batch for the complete import and process.

The Notifications sent to users are silent in that they don't pop up on the screen to avoid being intrusive. The arrival of Notifications is indicated by an increase in the notification flag count at the top-right of TG JE screens. Additionally, Notifications are aggregated to avoid recipients from being overwhelmed.

Comparing new SOH values against thresholds

The new real-time SOH values are compared against thresholds defined within TG JE. When the new SOH goes beyond a threshold, the outstanding need for the item is calculated, and an order automatically generated ready for review. These new orders are treated by TG JE as if they were manually processed.

The thresholds are recorded at individual inventory. The thresholds are:

  • time based: If the system’s current time exceeds this value, a recalculation is triggered. The value is set to be 1440 minutes (24 hours) from the last known Engine run date or today.

  • value based: If SOH falls below this value, a recalculation is triggered. The value is set to be an Inventory’s End Of Period Safety Stock amount for their current time period (i.e this week or this month).

  • forced flag based: When true, a recalculation is always triggered. This is set to true when there is no recommended projection data for an inventory for their current time period (i.e. this week).

A CUSP hook has been provided to grant customers the ability to set the Forced Flag, Time, Value entries as they see fit. This does not grant the ability to change how the thresholds are evaluated. For Value based checks, an Inventory’s SOH is always used. However, instead of EOP Safety Stock being the value, it may be another value provided by a more complex calculation.

For example, an item has 100 SOH at the start of the day and a defined safety stock level of 20. TG JE receives a continuous feed from TG Onera with updates showing the item is continuing to sell briskly. At a certain point during the day, an update is received with a SOH value of 19. As the SOH has dropped below the defined safety stock level of 20 the item is processed and an order is generated.

Allocation and replenishment items

Once a threshold has been crossed, a replenishment runs or an Allocation strategy runs (if there are multiple strategies defined for the item, only the smallest strategy ID runs).

A replenishment item is the item master associated with that replenishment. The item master runs using defined system policies.

These allocations and replenishments are placed into new specific Workload Queues (made for this purpose) that only handle intra-day processed items. Items that are queued multiple times are grouped together up to the point when the calculation runs. This helps reduce the noise associated with items with constantly changing stock levels. The 2 new Workload Queues are:

  • Real-time Retail Allocations

  • Real-time Retail Replenishment

Stock to Service curves

Stock to Service (STS) curves are analytical curves that relate each service level value with the average stock that is statistically required to achieve it.

Statistical demand characteristics are generated in terms of: volume (expected demand), variability (volatility of demand), frequency (typical market/customer order profile).

The STS curve therefore determines the relative average stock and the associated control stock values for each item/warehouse and for the desired service level.

Average delay [periods] Average supply delay added to the minimum lead-time
Minimum supply lot [units] (*) The minimum quantity that can be ordered from the supplier or produced .

Incremental supply lot [units] Size of the incremental quantities allowed, in addition to the minimum lot

2022.4

TG JE v2022.4 introduces an optional integration (via a Webhook) with a Stock-to-Service calculation that defines the right level of inventory based on historical and forecast sales data. This Stock-to-Service calculation is an analytical curve developed by TG Service Optimizer 99+ (SO99+) affecting the safety stock days of applied inventories.

This new calculation is in addition to TG JE's existing target service level calculation and applies to all previous order policy types that use Target Service Level such as Normal Stock and Redundant order policy types. This affects normal and redundant stocks because it targets safety stock which can be set in either Policy on a filter of items, or it could be set on individual inventories in Inventory Manager and Policy Information (via the Policy Information blade).

The Stock-to-Service calculation is a change from a fixed stock service level to an optimized service level. The result is that the Target Service Level itself can automatically change between 0% to 99% service level in accordance with policy restrictions causing the actual achievable service level to differ from a user's input due to constraints.

For example, a user inputs a desired service level of 90% in the Target Service Level field on the Inventory Manager screen. However, maximum stock is set in such a way that the maximum possible service level achievable is 75%. In this scenario, the value in the Target Service Level field automatically adjusts to 75%. The automatic adjustment prevents any false expectation on a user's part that they are satisfying a 90% service level when the maximum achievable is 75%. This automatic adjustment occurs in near real-time giving quick and clear visibility that a setting is preventing the desired Target Service Level which is not as easy to see with a Policy Task.

However, if an Inventory Planning Policy batch process has been configured as part of a policy task, then the automatic adjustment to an achievable service level, that is different from the user's input, occurs when the batch process is run and may not be immediately visible. When the batch process runs depends on all the policy tasks that were set up. The Policy Task only runs when started by a nightly job or from the Policy module when the user clicks Execute.

Constraint example

For inventory with no sales history or forecast, regardless of the values input in the UI, the system would automatically set demand parameters at 0 for the inventory and the calculated Target Service Level at 0% since there is nothing to satisfy.

Defining default pricing relationships across currencies

Within Assortment Planning (AP), pricing decisions are important. Pricing might vary from one Cluster to another, and the complexities of various currencies and exchange rates also make pricing complex. ToolsGroup JustEnough has previously supported pricing strategies, without having to manage pricing individually, through the Price Point Matrix screen (Price Matrix module).

AP now provides better support for planning in multiple currencies through an alternate process, Price Matrix, to manage pricing. Instead of defining translation rules, users define pricing relationships across currencies by means of a matrix.

Home AUT and Blended AUT

AP now distinguishes between two different types of AUT (or Average Unit Ticket, for example, the ticket price intended to be printed on merchandise tags).

The first of these, Home AUT, represents the AUT value for the country and currency of the defined Home Site as set in the Manage Assortment Plans screen for AP. This is the starting point for planning AUT, as this value is user-editable. Home AUT is only editable - and only has a value - in future periods. From this, Blended AUT is derived.

Blended AUT is considered to be a true representation of the expected earnings for a given item. Blended AUT incorporates several components, each of which is enabled by system policy:

  • VAT Rate changes between the Home Site and sites within each cluster

  • Price Exception Factors

  • Price Matrix conversions

Only Price Exceptions OR Price Matrix conversion can be enabled at once (but both can be disabled altogether if desired). VAT Rate changes cannot be enabled if Price Matrix conversions are enabled.

Blended AUT is derived in future periods from Home AUT. In past periods, as Home AUT has no values, Blended AUT is derived from provided sales history.

These two metrics are available in the following views: Collection, Placeholder, Cluster Sets, Multi-Currency Price View, Sales Plan, and Top Down Core Plan. Additionally, these metric have been added to the following read-only report views: Assortment Rollup and Reconciliation.

Price Matrix functionality

If a user enables Price Matrix conversions via system policy Price Conversion Method, and sets a Price Matrix on a given placeholder, then the configured Price Matrix is used to convert the provided Home AUT value to Blended AUT.

  • an entry in the matrix for the selling sites' country / currencies

Changing Sales plans while holding existing permanent markdown values

Hybrid Dual-Markdown plans (Cost and Retail) allow organizations to plan their Permanent and Promotional markdowns separately either as a dollar value or a percentage. Adjustments can be made to the Total dollar value or percentage, and those changes drill down to BOTH Promotion and Permanent markdowns, while holding the relationship between them. A consequence is that a change at the Total markdown level will adjust a previously entered Permanent markdown dollar value.

This is especially true for Hybrid Retail Dual-Markdown Plan (HRDM) plan type where markdowns affect EOP/BOP.

The locking functionality for the Hybrid Dual-Markdown type has been updated to better reflect the desired business case where users adjust the Sales plan but do not want to change the Permanent markdown values (which are planned based on stock price depreciation of stock on hand to be marked down, rather than affected by the Sales).

For TG JE v2023.1, Markdowns (Perm) metrics ($/%) can still be directly edited as desired, however, changes to a Sales plan no longer update the Markdowns (Perm) dollar metric.

Now, when a user has planned markdown values ($/%) for both Promotion and Permanent markdowns and adjusts the Sales plan for that period:

  • Markdowns (Promo) % will hold, and Markdowns (Promo) $ will adjust along with the Sales adjustment.

    The Markdowns (Promo) dollar value continues to update in relation to changes to the Sales plan as those metrics are interrelated.

  • Markdowns (Perm) $ will hold, and the Permanent markdown % will adjust along with the Sales adjustment.

  • Total markdown $/% will be updated based on the behavior of Promotion and Permanent markdowns as described.

If the desire is to update Total markdowns while holding the Markdowns (Perm) $ values, users should update the Markdowns (Promo) ($/%) metrics, rather than Total markdown metrics.

This change to how the Markdowns (Perm) metric is impacted when users change Sales or Sales Units (or other metrics) introduces a Breaking Change. For more information, see the Breaking Changes section.

Generalization Of component metrics

MFP has three sets of metrics called “component metrics". This means that they are corresponding parts of a whole other metric. In particular, the metric set Comp and Non-Comp are parts of Sales; the metric set of Regular, Promo, and Markdown also are parts of Sales; and the newly added Target Receipts 1 and 2 are parts of Target Receipts. The application now allows configurability as to which of these components are enabled and what they are called.

 

The Component metrics have been added to the Generic Metric Configuration view. Using machine learning to improve forecasting

ToolsGroup JustEnough v2022.3 introduces a new Forecasting Method labeled Gradient Boosting which is based on machine learning. The machine learning algorithm uses Gradient Boosted Decision Trees to analyze all relevant historical data (known as features) relating to demand patterns (e.g. seasonality, brands), business decisions (e.g. Promotions, changes in display and space), and external factors (e.g. holidays, footfall, weather). The historical data is then translated into an extremely accurate prediction or forecast.

One major feature of Gradient Boosting is that it is optimized for sparse data. For example, where an item has very low sales but is associated with an item with very high sales. Subtle or obscured connections between features can be uncovered leading to more accurate forecasts and improved sales. Additionally, machine learning requires fewer resources while leveraging all data for each series, is better at aggregate levels and handling Causals, and is easy to scale.

For more information, ToolsGroup Professional Services can reference Gradient Boosting.

The Trainer API generates fit models. The Inference API uses the fit models generated by the Trainer API to make a forecast. When users run an inventory forecast, the Forecast API gets any predictions and tournaments them against other statistical forecast methods to determine the best fit.   • can contain multiple Placeholders, so long as the original packing multiple for each is maintained within the multi-SKU prepack.

Using approximate stock quantities to assess recommended choices

Within the Target Worksheet and Target New Choices grids, recommended placeholders include Sales $ and Sales U targets. The stock needed to meet those Sales targets is not included in the Target Creation results due to Receipts being calculated later in the Assortment Planning process when other metrics such as Receipt eligible weeks, Presentation Minimums, Allow Receipts after Markdown, etc. are considered.

However, visibility to an approximate receipt or stock plan would greatly help planners decide whether to proceed with a choice recommendation. For example, planners may have an idea of the number of sizes needed per placeholder as a setup quantity. Depending on the store count within a Cluster Set/Cluster and the number of sizes needed for a presentation, the recommended Sales might not justify buying that choice, because the stock needed to setup is much greater than the Sales potential.

The target segment configuration section and the Target Worksheet now include the following two user editable metrics:

  • Target S/T %: Target Sales to Total Target Stock

  • Min Pres: The initial value is seeded from Hierarchy Defaults in AP mode. In standalone mode, the values do not seed. Only Hierarchy Defaults are used. Hierarchy Defaults by Cluster and Hierarchy Defaults by Time are not used.

Target S/T % and Min Pres can be defaulted at the segment level in the segment definitions widget, and both values can be overwritten at the cluster set / cluster level in the Target Worksheet.

Five new read-only Approximate Stock Unit metrics have been added to the Target Worksheet and Target New Choices grids. The metrics in the Target Worksheet are an aggregate for all new styles while in the Target New Choices grid, the metrics show values per choice.

To support differing strategic approaches to target stock levels at different organizations or product levels, there are two sets of metrics added to show approximate stock levels at stores at regular prices for new choices.

Firstly, approximate stock levels are calculated based solely on sell-through (ST%) targets without concern for maintaining minimum presentation throughout regular price selling periods. In this scenario, saleable stock is gradually depleted by sales throughout the regular price selling period, which may not be an issue where shoppers have lots of choices and are willing to substitute one choice for another. This approach helps avoid having excess inventory at the end of the regular price selling period for all styles. The metrics supporting this logic are:

  • Approx Stock Units (S/T %): TY Act New Sales Units / Target Sell Through %

  • Approx Stock Units By Site (S/T %): Approx Stock Units (S/T%) / TY Plan Site Count

Secondly, approximate stock levels are calculated with the requirements to have enough stock to meet sales and maintain a full minimum presentation throughout the regular price selling period. The metrics supporting this logic are:

  • Approx Stock Units (Min Pres): TY Act New Sales Units + (TY Plan Site Count * Min Pres)

  • Approx Stock Units By Site (Min Pres): Approx Stock Units (Min Pres) / TY Plan Site Count

  • Sales To Approx Stock Units (Min Pres): TY Act New Sales Units / Approx Stock Units (Min Pres)

An approximate stock target based on Sales plus Min Pres does not respond to a defined ST% target. The Sales To Approx Stock Units (Min Pres) metric is an Approximate ST% allowing for a comparison of the result of the (Min Pres) calculations to an ST%.

All 5 metrics are available for use as needed. If some of these metrics are not useful based on the strategy in place, those metrics can be hidden using the Fields selector.

Improved control over one-off and poor performing variants

For TG JE v2023.2, the functionality associated with data cleansing in AP and Target Choice Count has been expanded with the addition of a new threshold and new fields that give users more control over one-off variants and habitual poor performers.

The new data cleansing threshold, Active Variant Total Minimum, lets users identify and remove consistently low sellers from the cleansing process. As with the other existing thresholds the default is set using a system policy. In this case, new system policy Cleanse: Active Variant Total Minimum (Module: Target Choice Count). The default is used when segments are created. However, users can change the value at the segment level.

The Active Variant Total Minimum functionality:

  • finds the total cleansed Active sales for all variants in the segment / cluster set / cluster

  • excludes any variants whose total is lower than this Minimum

Choosing whether to rationalize placeholders

A new read-only checkbox labeled Carryover allows users to decide whether to include or exclude given placeholders from processing. If an item is to be carried over, but is near the end of its life and thus has a minimal sales plan in the current time period, then users can include the item so that Rationalization is informed by the item’s performance and recommends new placeholders to take its place. If the item is a carryover but it is expected to continue to sell with the same volume as last year, then the item can be excluded because its plan is already created and accounted for.

The method for population of the Carryover field depends on whether Target Creation is accessed through AP or standalone Target Choice Count:

  • In AP, Carryover is based on whether the placeholder’s phase dates intersect both the scenario Window of Time and the Comparison time period. For example, if the Comparison time period is over 2022 week 1 - week 10, and the scenario is over 2023 week 1 - week 10, an item selling from 2022 week 5 to 2023 week 15 will be considered carryover, but an item that phases out in 2022 week 30 is not.

  • In standalone Target Choice Count, Carryover is calculated with the same logic as in AP, but uses Inventory level phase dates instead of placeholder phase dates. If at least one of the variant’s inventories' phase dates intersect both Window of Time and Comparison time frames, then the variant is considered carryover.

Excluding placeholders from rationalization post data cleansing

A new editable field labeled Inclusion Type gives users the ability, even after data cleansing, to manually exclude some placeholders from consideration in the rationalization process. For example, a placeholder was a very unique item whose trends are not expected to be repeated in the future. The default setting is Full Process.

Setting Definition Possible Use Case
Full Process The variant will be included in full Rationalization processing, including in Data Cleansing. This is what the majority of items will fall into: non-carryover items that are a solid representation of the expected productivity and relationship between best to worst selling items.
Target Set Only The variant will be included in Data Cleansing to set Target values, but will be excluded from the Rationalization process. The item is a carryover item which should be included in Comparison Active data and target setting, but since it is already included in carryover, it should not be used for rationalization. The item was a strong seller last year due to intense societal trends, but it is not a repeatable scenario this year, so rationalization should not be informed by such a productive item.
Exclude The variant will not be included in any scenario processing. It will not influence Target or Recommended values. An item that should not have been selling in the cluster, so there is no desire to model it in the future.

Including items in data cleansing but excluding them from rationalization

Include Carryover In Rationalization?: Default is checked. If unchecked, then during the Calculate process, items flagged as Carryover are automatically set to Inclusion Type Target Set Only. They will be included in data cleansing but not in rationalization. This should be set if most carryover items in the Segment are long-life items that will continue to sell similarly to last year.

Selective creation of site (store)-level MFP plans

When creating plans in MFP, users can decide which levels of the location hierarchy should be included in the plan. In general, the recommendation is to not plan at the lowest level (aka Store or Site) as that level of granularity is generally not needed. Also, if the plan is established at the site-level, all MFP data must be stored at that level. This results in large amounts of data to be saved and reduced performance within MFP.

However, MFP plans can be created at the site-level, if the user desires. With data requirements and performance speed in mind, focused store plans should only be created for specific (limited) sites.

Using sites (store)-level MFP plans to create site clusters

This allows the machine-learning algorithms within Site Clustering to reference site-level planned data to create clusters. Creating site clusters based on site-level planned data supports clustering based on planned MFP-site performance, rather than historical site performance.

Calculating lost sales for web stores without SOH

Lost sales for both physical and web-based stores that maintain their own stock on hand (SOH) is calculated using the store's SOH. This wasn't possible for web-based stores that do not maintain their own SOH. The existing lost sale calculation functionality has been extended to enable the SOH of the primary source site to be used to calculate lost sales.

Now, sites accumulate lost sales for a period if:

  • there is a proper supply chain

  • the primary source site has zero historical SOH

  • the site has a positive effective forecast

  • lost sale is greater than forecast sales.

To select a site that needs the SOH of the primary source site to be used, check the check box in the Calculate Lost Sales Using Source Site? field in the Site Manager Document grid and click Save.

This new field does not display by default. To add this new field, click Choose on the Site Manager Document toolbar and include the Calculate Lost Sales Using Source Site? option from the new Site Lost Sales metadata group.

A filter can be created to display only those sites that are using the primary source site to calculate lost sales.

The default supply chain that is used is configured using a nightly task or by setting a default using system policy Supply Chain Default Active Supply Chain ID (Module: Supply Chain). A supply chain specific to web stores that do not maintain their own SOH can be set.
